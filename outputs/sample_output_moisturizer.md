## 2. Risk Flags

- Customer reviews indicate repeated reports of skin irritation, redness, burning, and stinging sensations, particularly among users with sensitive skin. These safety-like complaints require human review.
- Multiple complaints about greasy texture and slow absorption suggest a formulation issue that may affect user experience and satisfaction.
- Several customers mention strong fragrance as a negative factor, indicating a possible mismatch with customer preferences or expectations.
- Packaging issues including leakage and pump malfunction were reported, which could impact product usability and customer satisfaction.

## 3. Recommended Actions

1. Escalate skin irritation and sensitivity complaints to a qualified human safety reviewer for further assessment.
2. Review formulation with R&D to investigate causes of greasy texture and slow absorption, considering adjustments for lighter feel.
3. Evaluate fragrance concentration and consider offering a fragrance-free variant or reducing scent strength.
4. Investigate packaging quality control focusing on leakage and pump mechanism reliability.
5. Review product page language to ensure accurate communication about fragrance and texture to better align customer expectations.

## 4. Manager-Ready Memo

Based on an analysis of 15 recent customer reviews for HydraGlow Daily Moisturizer, there are multiple reports of skin irritation and discomfort, especially among sensitive skin users, which require human safety review. Additionally, customers frequently mention a greasy texture and strong fragrance as detractors, alongside packaging issues such as leakage and pump problems. These findings highlight areas for product formulation and packaging improvements as well as potential adjustments in marketing communication. All conclusions are drawn from subjective customer feedback and should be validated through further human review before any final decisions.

## 5. Human Review Checklist

- Verify the extent and nature of skin irritation complaints without assuming clinical causation.
- Confirm whether reported burning, redness, and stinging are consistent and significant enough to warrant formulation review.
- Assess if fragrance strength complaints align with product claims and labeling.
- Examine packaging failure reports to determine if they indicate a systemic quality control issue.
- Ensure no over-interpretation of subjective reviews as medical or safety proof.
- Recommend additional data collection if evidence is insufficient for conclusive action.
